Land Transport Fatalities Across Australia States

Visualisation 2 Assignment Completed by Leow Ziping
Australia is among the top 25 in ranking when it comes to worst road safety performance, having an rate of 4.54 fatality per 100,000 total population. Although the rate has declined by a significant 34% from 2009-2018, land transport accidents were still ranked 9th as the leading cause of fatality in Australia that should continually be reduced.

Fatality rate across Australia States

Fatality rates in Northern Territory is unconventionally the highest at 9.3 fatality per 100,000. Fatality rate in Australian Capital Territory is just second to them at 5.32, making it a worrying figure as both figures are higher than the national average. The high rates could be brought about by the inconsistent and rocky terrains or even bad driving ethics and therefore it is essential that road safety should be tightly monitored by government and consider building safer road infrastructures for the people there.


Fortunately fatality rates amongst the denser states like New South Wales, Victoria and Queensland averaged to be at 2.51 fatality per 100,000, making it below the national average. It is definitely useful if drivers or travellers are able to take higher precaution amongst themselves and everyone can do their part to reduce the fatality rate moving forward.

Fatality Trend Across Australia States

Fatality rate is exponentially decreasing over the last 20 years, with close to 1900 fatalities per year in the early 2000s drop to just 1100 fatalities in 2021. There is even 2 sharp drops from 2001-2002 period and even in 2020, providing a positive outlook it shows that government has been ramping up efforts to improve road infrastructures and safety.

Fatality rate monitored weekly

Fatality rate has been monitored on a daily basis to better reflect the fatality pattern and it comes as no surprise that the weekend makes up for almost half the fatalities. With an established nightlife and leading towards the weekend, people in general tend to go out for night activities and it is important that government increase their checks on weekends to prevent these prevailing rates to have a better grasp of the road safety issue as it is correlated to drink driving, one of the major leading causes of road fatality.

Age and Gender Fatality Count

Since 2000, the age group with the highest % of fatalities has always been the middle aged group (40-64), followed by the early adulthood group (26-39). Males also make up more than 60% of fatalities in every state. South Australia recorded 84.61% of males fatalities in just 2021 whereas for Females, the highest fatality rate for 2021 comes from New South Wales with a record 36%. Government should allocate their focus on a targeted demographic to improve on road awareness and safety protocols to reduce fatality rates

Road User Fatality Count

Car drivers certainly made up at least 40% of the fatality count with car related accidents for road users while Motorcycle riders make up similar fatality counts as passengers of car drivers, which indicates the motorcycle related accidents are second to car. However, the overall fatalities for these vehicle related accidents have come down over the years which is not so worrying. What is surprising is the pedal cyclist fatalities. Despite it being a fraction of the total fatalities, it is the only road user group that has not decline over the years and this is an indication that perhaps government should implement road safety awareness courses as more people are resorting to cycling as a mode of transportation in the recent years.
